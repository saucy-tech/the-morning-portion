> And when the day of Pentecost was fully come, they were all with one accord in one place. -- Acts 2:1 (KJV)

The people in the upper room were not legends when they gathered. Mary was there. Jesus' brothers were there. The apostles were there with memories of fear, denial, confusion, and scattered courage. About one hundred and twenty people kept coming before God together.

They could not make the sound from heaven arrive. They could not script tongues like as of fire. They could not manufacture the moment when the Holy Ghost would fill the house.

They could be in one place. They could continue in prayer. They could stay in one accord until God did what only God could do.

## Before the Sound

Pentecost did not begin with public boldness. It began with hidden agreement. The witnesses who would speak to nations first learned to wait together in a room. Their unity did not mean every question had vanished. It meant they brought themselves under the same promise.

That kind of unity still costs something. Pride has to loosen its grip. Hurt has to come into prayer instead of staying in private rehearsal. Preference has to bow before the promise of God.

One accord is not a mood. It is surrendered people seeking the same Lord.

## What You Lead With

Most of us step into the day carrying a role: worker, parent, teacher, friend, leader, helper. Roles matter, but they cannot become the deepest name we answer to. In Christ, you are a witness before you are a title. The Holy Ghost does not fill a job description. He fills a person.

Ask what you are leading with as you enter the room God has assigned to you. If anxiety fills you, anxiety will leak out. If pride fills you, pride will find a way to speak. If the Spirit fills you, even ordinary words can carry life.

Gather your heart before God before you gather yourself for the task. The room needs more than your outline. It needs a witness with oil in the lamp.
