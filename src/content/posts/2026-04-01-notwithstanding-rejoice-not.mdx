> "Notwithstanding in this rejoice not, that the spirits are subject unto you; but rather rejoice, because your names are written in heaven." — Luke 10:20 (KJV)

The seventy came back buzzing. They had gone out with nothing — no money, no backup plan, total dependence — and it had worked. Demons obeyed when they used Jesus' name. Evil spirits fled. They had authority they didn't fully understand, and it was intoxicating.

Jesus didn't deflate them. He said: yes, I saw it. I watched Satan fall like lightning. The power is real, and you have it.

And then He changed the subject.

## The Redirect

"Don't rejoice because spirits submit to you," He said. "Rejoice because your names are written in heaven."

That's a significant correction. Not a rebuke — a redirect. He wasn't telling them the victories didn't matter. He was telling them they were building their joy on the wrong foundation.

Power is seductive because it's visible. You can feel it working. You can point to it. The demon fled, the sick person was healed, the crowd was amazed — these are measurable wins, and they feel good. There's nothing wrong with that feeling. But Jesus knew what happens when visible outcomes become the scoreboard: you start measuring your worth by your results. A good week feels like closeness to God; a hard week starts to feel like abandonment.

## The Right Scoreboard

What He offered instead is something that never fluctuates. Your name is written in heaven. Not because of what you accomplished last week, not because the last prayer you prayed produced results — because you belong to Him.

That's the only victory that doesn't have an off-season.

This matters more than it sounds. A lot of faith quietly runs on the fuel of outcomes. We pray and something happens, so we feel near to God. We pray and nothing seems to happen, so we wonder what's wrong. The disciples were on the verge of building that kind of faith — one where spiritual power was the evidence of God's favor. Jesus interrupted it before it took root.

## What Shifts When You Get This Right

You stop white-knuckling visible results. You stop needing every prayer to resolve cleanly or every act of obedience to be rewarded immediately. The ground under you isn't "did it work?" — it's "am I His?"

That question has a settled answer. And settled answers make for a different kind of person — one who can keep going when things are hard, who can shake the dust and move to the next harvest, who doesn't need a spectacular story to stay motivated.

Where have you been measuring your spiritual life by the wrong scoreboard? Not outcomes versus belonging — but are you clear on which one actually holds you?

That's worth sitting with today.
